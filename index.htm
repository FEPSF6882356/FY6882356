High Itensity Taining:

HIT method requires short quick bursts of intense training done at 70-90% of one’s maximum effort capabilities, a good example of such can be high intensity interval training (HIIT) which includes; sprinting, CrossFit, plyometrics and circuit training. One of the greatest advantages of HIT is that it is incredibly time efficient, requiring only 20-30 minutes per day and offering substantial health benefits in terms of calorie expenditure, “enhancing cardiovascular function” , “Increasing VO2 max (maximum oxygen uptake)”and “Enhanced metabolic efficiency of muscle tissue”.  Little, J.P. et al. (2010,p. 2-7)

Among the Important keys to high intensity training is the excess post-exercise oxygen consumption or EPOC. Which referrers to the body continuing to “impact fuel utilization for up to 24 hours post-exercise” as oxygen levels and muscle tissue is regulated while metabolic waste is removed. This makes the training highly ideal for individuals whom wish to maximise fat loss in the shortest period of time. [Collins, A., 2020. The Afterburn Effect:]

High intensity training also increases the VO2 max of a person which enables them to preform better in activities that require quick releases of energy, such as weightlifting and or competitive sports. Proof of this can be seen on the graph below of 61 participants which shows that high intensity interval training (HIIT) leads to greater improvements in VO2 max compared to moderate-intensity continuous training. Common injury’s include muscle strains, joint problems and cardiovascular stress. So the proper warm ups and cool downs / adequate recovery are essential to avoid overtraining, else a sharp decline in performance and increased risk of injury occur. However when done correctly there can be “rapid muscle growth and strength increases” with in the first year of training as well as “Quick improvements in neuromuscular coordination” , this is commonly known as newbie gains.[ A Systematic Review with Meta-Analysis of the Effect of Resistance Training]
